How it works

How we turn market "insurance" into your income

Three simple ideas power everything we do. No finance degree required.

  1. 1

    We collect a premium up front

    Like a car-insurance company, we get paid cash the moment we take on a risk. That money lands in the fund immediately — before we know how the market will move.

  2. 2

    We stay balanced, not directional

    We build positions that can win across a wide range of outcomes — market up a little, down a little, or flat. We're not betting on a direction, so we don't need a crystal ball.

  3. 3

    Time quietly pays us

    The protection we sold loses value a little every day it isn't needed. If the market behaves, that premium melts in our favor — and becomes return for you.

Income strategy

The Wheel — rinse and repeat income

One of our favourite tools: a self-renewing cycle that collects premium at every turn. Instead of hoping a stock goes up, we get paid just for being willing to own it — and then paid again while we do.

🎯

Step 1 — Sell a cash-secured put

We pick a stock at a price we'd happily own. Someone pays us a premium for the right to make us buy at that price. Cash in hand, immediately.

📉

Step 2 — If assigned, we own it

If the stock drops to our strike price, we buy it — but at a discount (we already collected the put premium). We're not upset; this was always an option we priced in.

📞

Step 3 — Sell a covered call

Now we own the stock, we sell someone the right to buy it from us at a higher price. More premium collected. We're getting paid to wait.

🔄

Step 4 — Called away or repeat

If the stock rises above our call strike, it's sold at a profit — plus we keep all premiums. Then we start the Wheel again on the next candidate.

Pair Trading — profit from the relationship, not the direction

Most investors bet on whether a stock goes up or down. Pair trading ignores direction entirely — we bet on the relationship between two assets returning to normal. It works in rising markets, falling markets, and flat ones.

🔗

Step 1 — Find a historically linked pair

We identify two assets that move together over time — think SPY vs. QQQ, or two competing companies in the same sector. The link is tested statistically (cointegration), not just eyeballed.

📐

Step 2 — Measure the spread

We track the price difference between the two assets as a Z-score. When they drift more than ~2 standard deviations apart, history says they're overdue to reconnect.

↔️

Step 3 — Buy the laggard, short the leader

When the spread widens, we buy the underperformer and short-sell the overperformer in equal dollar amounts. If the pair reconnects, both legs profit — regardless of whether the market moves up or down.

✅

Step 4 — Close when the spread converges

Once the gap closes back to normal (Z-score near 0), both positions are closed. Profit comes from the convergence, not from predicting market direction. We then watch for the next divergence.
